Part 1: Industry Business Logic & Competitive Evolution
From Industry Expert & Strategic Investor Perspective
1.1 Industry Definition & Value Chain

What It Is: Core products/services and technology base
What It Does: Key functions and customer value proposition
Value Chain: Upstream suppliers → Production → Downstream applications
Profit Pools: Where value concentrates across the chain

1.2 Last Upcycle (时间段)
Supply-Demand Structure

Supply: Capacity expansion, new entrants, cost inflation
Demand: End market drivers, volume growth, pricing power
Balance: Tight supply, allocation mode, margin expansion

Competitive Dynamics

Market share shifts and concentration changes
Winners/losers and underlying drivers
Entry barriers and competitive moats

Strategic Response

Corporate actions: Capex, M&A, vertical integration
New market development and applications
Long-term positioning moves

1.3 Recent Downcycle (时间段)
Supply-Demand Structure

Supply: Overcapacity, cost pressure, consolidation
Demand: End market weakness, demand destruction, elasticity test
Balance: Oversupply, inventory destocking, price erosion

Competitive Dynamics

Market share redistribution during stress
Survivor characteristics vs exits
Barrier effectiveness under pressure

Strategic Response

Defensive moves: Cost cuts, capacity rationalization
Offensive plays: Market share grab, strategic acquisitions
Pivot strategies: New applications, geographies

1.4 Current State & Forward Outlook
Supply-Demand Structure

Current equilibrium and utilization rates
Supply discipline vs demand recovery signals
Structural vs cyclical factors

Competitive Position

Post-cycle competitive landscape
Consolidated market structure
New entrant threats

Future Trajectory

Next cycle catalysts and timing
Emerging growth vectors
Permanent structural shifts
